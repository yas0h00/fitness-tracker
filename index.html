💡 Sleep Science for Students
7–8 hours is non-negotiable. Less than 6h = 24% more hunger hormone (ghrelin) next day = 300–400 extra kcal eaten without realizing.
Same bedtime daily trains your circadian rhythm. Sleeping at 10:30 PM every night = waking up alert at 6 AM naturally within 2 weeks.
Memory is consolidated during sleep. Everything you studied gets filed into long-term memory in the last 2 hours of sleep. Cutting sleep = cutting memory.

⚡ The 5 Rules That Do 80% of the Work
🕘
No food after 8:30 PM
Biggest single change. Late night eating converts directly to fat because metabolism slows. Even if you're hungry, drink warm water or herbal tea.
🍵
Max 2 chai + zero cold drinks
4 cups chai with sugar = 400 empty kcal/day. Cold drinks = 150 kcal per bottle. Cutting both removes ~500 kcal daily with zero effort. Replace with black coffee or green tea.
🍚
Half the rice/roti + eat salad first
Eat cucumber/onion/tomato salad BEFORE rice. It fills stomach with fibre so you eat 30% less carbs naturally. Cut rice from plate-full to 1 small bowl.
🚶
20-min walk every study break
Already in your study algorithm. At your weight, walking burns 120–140 kcal per 20 minutes. 3 breaks/day = 360–420 kcal burned just from breaks. Don't skip.
😴
7 hours sleep minimum — same time daily
Sleep deprivation increases ghrelin (hunger hormone) by 24% — you'll eat 300–400 extra kcal next day just from being tired. Also kills memory consolidation built during study sessions.
